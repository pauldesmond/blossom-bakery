How to update your website

A simple guide for keeping the website fresh — no code, no stress, just a few clicks whenever you'd like to change something.

Bookmark this in your browser

myblossombakery.com/edit-blossom/editor.html

This is your editor. Open it whenever you'd like to change something.

  1. 1

    Open the editor

    Click the link above. You'll see your website on the left, and a panel of tools on the right. The list of pages — Homepage, Wedding Cakes, Scones, and so on — sits down the left-hand side.

  2. 2

    Pick a page to edit

    Click any page in the list. The preview updates so you can see exactly what visitors see.

    Tip — Want to take a page off the website temporarily? Hover over it in the list and click the little toggle to hide it. You can switch it back on any time.
  3. 3

    Make your changes

    Click on anything in the preview to edit it:

    • Click a piece of text — change the wording in the panel on the right.
    • Click a photo — upload a new one from your computer.

    Don't worry about getting it perfect first time — nothing is live until you say so.

  4. 4

    Save your draft

    Click Save Draft at the top of the editor. Your work is safely tucked away in the browser — you can close the tab and come back tomorrow exactly where you left off.

    Good to know — Drafts are saved on this computer in this browser. If you switch devices, you'll need to export your draft (next step) to bring it with you.
  5. 5

    Export your draft

    When you're happy with everything, click Export Draft. A small file lands in your Downloads folder — something like blossom-draft-2026-05-09.json.

    Don't worry about what's inside it — it's just a tidy parcel of all your changes.

  6. 6

    Send it to Paul

    Email or message that file to Paul. He'll review it, click a button to publish, and your changes will appear on the live website within a couple of minutes.

    Why this step? — It gives a friendly second pair of eyes before anything goes live — useful for catching the odd typo or wonky photo. It's also your safety net: nothing reaches the website without your sign-off being confirmed.
  7. +

    Want to add a brand-new page?

    Scroll to the bottom of the page list and click + New page (duplicate & fill). Give your new page a name (something like Christmas Cookies), pick an existing page to use as a template — its layout becomes your starting point — and click Create draft page.

    Your new page appears in the list with a small pink NEW badge. Edit it just like any other page — click on text to change words, click on photos to swap them.

    Hidden by default — New pages stay hidden from your main menu until they're ready. When you'd like to show it, mention it to Paul when you send your draft and he'll add it to the menu for you.

That's it. No passwords to remember, nothing to install — just edit, save, send.
